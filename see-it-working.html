01 · See it working

See it working

Talk is cheap and AI demos are usually theatre. This one is deliberately unglamorous: the same difficult task, done twice.

This demonstration uses one example: a customer complaint email. The same approach fits quotes, exceptions, compliance checks, onboarding paperwork, whatever judgement-heavy task already eats your team's time.

02 · The task

The task

A complaint email from an unhappy customer. The kind that needs judgement: an apology that doesn't admit the wrong thing, a policy applied correctly, history taken into account, and a tone that doesn't make it worse. The kind of email a good member of staff spends half an hour on and a tired one gets wrong.

04 · Round two, in full

What "done properly" actually checks

  • It checks first. The customer's history, the relevant policy, the business's own rules about tone and what can be offered.
  • It shows its working. The draft arrives with what was checked and why it says what it says, visible.
  • It knows what it doesn't know. Where the records don't answer something, it says so and asks, instead of inventing a confident answer.
  • It stops. The draft waits for a person. Someone in the business reviews it, sees the evidence, changes what they want and approves it. Nothing was ever going out on its own.

05 · What you're actually looking at

What you're actually looking at

This is a demonstration, built to show the approach. It isn't a client's system and I don't claim it is. It is built the same way as the systems I run in a real business every working day: records in, rules applied, evidence shown, person in charge. What changes for your business is the workflow, the records and the rules. The discipline is the constant.

The part that matters for you

The point of the first conversation isn't to book a project. It's to work out which of your workflows would benefit from this treatment, and then show you, on your kind of problem, what round two looks like for you.
