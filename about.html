About

I'm Stuart Cadger. I build and run governed AI systems inside a real UK business as my day job, and Fine Kilter is my own practice: the same discipline, offered to small service businesses that want work fixed properly without buying into hype.

02 · The longer version

The longer version

My background is business systems and revenue operations: the unglamorous middle of a working SME, where processes actually live. For years my job has been making operational work run properly: designing the systems, building the automation, and lately designing, building and running AI-governed systems whose results get reported at board level.

That last part matters, because it's where my view of the AI market hardened. I watched the tools multiply and the promises inflate, and the businesses around me were being sold subscriptions, not solutions. The thing that actually works isn't the model. It's the system around the model: the records it draws on, the rules it follows, the checks it passes, and the person who stays in charge of the result. Models improve and get swapped out. The system is the asset, and it should belong to the business, not to a vendor.

Fine Kilter exists to build exactly that, for businesses too small to interest the big consultancies and too sensible to want a moonshot.

03 · The name

The name

"In fine kilter" is an old phrase for proper working order. The Scots root, kilt, meant the proper way of doing something. That's the whole promise: your business, running in fine kilter. And yes, I'm in Scotland, and no, there's no tartan anywhere on this site.

The stance, in one paragraph

I'd rather talk you out of a purchase than into one. Most small businesses don't need an AI strategy, a platform migration or another monthly licence. They need the two or three workflows that quietly eat their margin fixed properly, by someone who'll say "this bit isn't worth automating" when it isn't. That's the practice.
